SuFIA: Language-Guided Augmented Dexterity
for Robotic Surgical Assistants

Masoud Moghani1, Lars Doorenbos2, William Chung-Ho Panitch3,
Sean Huver4, Mahdi Azizian4, Ken Goldberg3, Animesh Garg1,4,5
 
1University of Toronto, 2University of Bern, 3University of California, Berkeley,
4NVIDIA, 5Georgia Institute of Technology
Paper (upon publication) Code (release upon publication)

Abstract

In this work, we present SuFIA, the first framework for natural language-guided augmented dexterity for robotic surgical assistants. SuFIA incorporates the strong reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs) with perception modules to implement high-level planning and low-level control of a robot for surgical sub-task executions. This enables a learning-free approach to implement partial surgical autonomy without any in-context examples or motion primitives. Additionally, we propose a human-in-the-loop approach, which enables SuFIA to restore control to the surgeon in the case of insufficient information, mitigating unexpected errors for mission-critical tasks. We evaluate SuFIA on four distinct surgical sub-tasks.
